01

How the reference site actually works

Before anyone quotes a build, one fact reframes the whole project: tobaccoroadtravel.com isn't a real-time booking store — it's a lead-and-quote "concierge" site. Visitors browse "3 rounds / 2 nights" bundles and submit a request; a small team replies with a custom itinerary and takes payment offline.

That model is far cheaper and faster to build than live e-commerce — and it's the right place for us to start. The site even runs on WordPress (its page URLs expose /wp-content/), which tells us the whole thing is achievable with off-the-shelf tooling. No custom platform required.

03

Three ways to build it

Recommendation first — launch lean this season, add live booking only once demand is proven.

✓ Recommended · v1

A · WordPress + quote forms

Mirrors exactly how Tobacco Road is built. Load hotels & packages as content; a form plugin routes quote requests to an inbox + CRM.

Fastest, cheapest, proven.

~$4k–$12klive in weeks
Higher polish

B · Custom build

Bespoke Next.js / React site. Worth it only if ATMA wants a distinctive, brand-forward experience beyond a theme.

Full design control; every edit needs a developer without a CMS.

~$15k–$40k+2–3× the cost
Real-time booking

C · White-label platform

A stay-and-play engine with live pricing and online payment — the fastest path to true checkout.

Needs firm room blocks + tee-sheet allocations. Overkill until volume warrants.

SaaS + rev-shareadd later

Who does the work is a separate lever — a local agency/freelancer, or a DIY build we drive ourselves. For a brand like ATMA's, a professional designer on the visuals is money well spent even if we assemble the rest.

04

What it actually takes

Timeline · Option A

2–4 weeks for an MVP (home + 2–3 packages + quote form + 2 hotels) once content is in hand. 6–10 weeks for a full v1.

Directional budget

Build: ~$4k–$12k · Hosting + plugins: ~$40–$120/mo · Photography (if new): ~$1k–$4k. For scoping, not a quote.

We'd gather — from ATMA

Hotel list, descriptions & photos, the rates or room-block allowance we can package, and who owns fulfillment.

From Finley / Carolina Golf

Course info & photography, tee-time availability & green-fee rates, and which partner courses to include.

The real work isn't the website. The site is a few weeks of design and content. The engine is the commercial agreement — revenue split on a bundled sale, room-block and tee-time commitments, who owns the booking, cancellation terms. Nail those and the website is the easy part. That's exactly the kind of partnership Finley builds.

05

Suggested phasing

2

All ATMA properties, course pages, photo galleries, downloadable travel guide, refined design.

Live booking — only if volume warrants

Add a stay-and-play engine with real-time pricing and online payment.

06

Recommended next steps

  1. 30-minute scoping call to agree on v1 scope and the 2–3 flagship packages.
  2. Confirm the commercial terms — revenue share, room blocks, tee-time allocation. The actual gating item.
  3. Gather content & photography from both sides.
  4. Engage a designer/developer for the Option A build; target a v1 launch ahead of peak golf-travel season.
